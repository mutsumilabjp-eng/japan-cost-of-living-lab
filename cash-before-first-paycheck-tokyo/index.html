How Much Cash Do You Need Before Your First Paycheck in Tokyo?

The dangerous number in a Tokyo move is often not the monthly rent. It is the cash that leaves your account before the first full salary arrives.

I'm Mutsumi, the editor behind Japan Cost of Living Lab. I separate this move into three clocks: the lease payment, the first-week setup, and payroll. If you put all three on one timeline, a cheap-looking apartment can become the riskier choice.

The short answer

Do not start with a generic savings target. Start with a written apartment quote, the date keys are handed over, and the employer's actual payroll cutoff and payday.

For a planning example, an ¥85,000 apartment with four months of rent-equivalent initial housing costs uses ¥340,000 before furniture, temporary accommodation, food, transport, or an emergency reserve. If the quote reaches six months, housing alone becomes ¥510,000. These are scenarios, not Tokyo-wide promises; the written quote decides your number.

A worked scenario

Suppose the apartment is ¥85,000 per month and the management fee is ¥5,000. The agent's estimate shows housing charges equal to four months of rent. You also plan ¥100,000 for essential setup and ¥120,000 for six weeks of non-rent living costs.

The visible subtotal is already ¥560,000 before any additional quoted fees or emergency reserve. The useful question is not “Is ¥85,000 cheap?” It is “What must be paid on each date, and what remains after that transfer?”

Ask payroll these four questions

  1. What is the monthly payroll cutoff date?
  2. What is the first payday for my start date?
  3. Will the first payment be a full month or a partial month?
  4. Which relocation, commute, or temporary-housing costs are reimbursed, and

    5. are they paid before or after I spend the money?

A salary offer can be sufficient over a year and still create a cash shortage in week three. Payday timing belongs in the housing decision.

Ask the agent for the written total

Request a line-by-line initial-cost estimate before treating a listing as affordable. Check deposit, key money, agency fee, guarantor fee, insurance, lock change, cleaning, first rent, prorated rent, management fee, and any optional service package. Ask which lines are required and which are optional.

Do not rely on a “three to six months” rule after you have a real quote. The range is only a planning prompt; the document is the decision input.

Keep the emergency reserve outside the move budget

If the plan spends every available yen on the lease and appliances, it does not contain an emergency reserve. Keep the reserve as a separate line after all scheduled move payments. The right amount depends on health, dependents, job stability, and access to support, so this guide does not prescribe one number.

Free one-apartment check

Before applying, write down:

  • Monthly rent and management fee
  • Total initial housing transfer
  • Keys date
  • First full payday
  • Setup purchases that cannot wait
  • Cash remaining after every scheduled payment

If any line is unknown, the apartment is not ready for a cash decision.
