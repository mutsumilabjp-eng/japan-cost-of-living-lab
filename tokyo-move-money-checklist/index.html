10-point Tokyo Move Money Checklist

Last reviewed: July 24, 2026.

Use this beside every apartment quote and salary offer. Do not rely on the advertised rent or a verbal payroll estimate.

Before you sign

  1. Deposit: Write the number of months and the yen amount.
  2. Key money: Confirm whether it applies and whether any part is refundable.
  3. Agency fee: Check the fee and consumption tax in writing.
  4. Guarantor company: Record the initial fee, renewal fee, and payment timing.
  5. Property-specific fees: Check insurance, lock change, cleaning, support service, and contract renewal charges.

Before you book the move

  1. Temporary lodging: Budget for the nights before keys are available.
  2. Setup cash: Price essential furniture, appliances, utility activation, and the first grocery shop.
  3. Payroll cutoff: Confirm the cutoff date, first pay date, and whether the first payment covers a full month.
  4. Employer reimbursements: Record what is covered and the date reimbursement will actually arrive.
  5. Emergency cash: Decide the minimum amount that must remain untouched after the contract and move.

One final check

Run a second version of the plan with one delayed paycheck and one unexpected property fee. If that version creates a cash gap, change the apartment, move date, or savings target before signing.
